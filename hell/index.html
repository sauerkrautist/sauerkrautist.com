Hell is allistic people

Content notice: Achtung Satire!

Don’t Explain the Joke they said. And then they don’t get the joke. So here’s infodumping the subtext of this site’s subtitle at length.1 To make this content more inclusive for persons without autism, we are prepeared to go very far out on a ledge and employ a diverse array of evidence-based psychological bonding strategies like person-first language (PFL), bold type in body copy, Oxford commas, and exclamation marks!

And mild xenophobia.2

Academics! Endnotes! It’s all Greek to me!

The term autism was coined in 1911 by a failed Swiss farmer called Eugen Bleuler.3 It is derived from the Greek word αὐτός, meaning self.

The term allism was coined in 2003 by a non-allistic English software engineer called Andrew Main.4 It is derived from the Greek word ἄλλος, meaning other.

The plot thickens!

Sartre: “Hell is other people”

Few people have actually seen, read or listened to Jean-Paul Charles Aymard Sartre’s seminal play Huis clos, but most people are familiar with its catchphrase L’enfer, c’est les autres.5

No single line in the history of playwriting has been as commonly misunderstood, and no single misunderstanding in the history of cognition has been as frequently mansplained.6

The situation was so grave that the man himself took to his fountain pen and thus provided a clarification that was promptly misinterpreted as well (emphasis boldy ours):7

It shall be left as an exercise to the astute reader to replace other with allistic and self/selves with autism upon rereading.8

We argue that Sartre’s authoritative treatise on the topos of his own diction, invoking a brisk crossfire of clever allusions to Greek pathological jargon that is hitherto unprecedented in the canon of existentialistic discourse, emerges as a relentless deconstruction of internalized ableism as an oppressive social construct that is facilitated by psychiatric diagnosis as the quintessential manifestation of human judgement, and thus reminds us that for autistic people, hell is indeed allistic people.9

QED.10
