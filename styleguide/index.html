The Elements of Autistic Style

With apologies to Robert Bringhurst.1

Interpunction

We never use exclamation points and avoid parentheses in prose unless they disclose the abbreviation for a preceding word, compound (C, CO, CPD, CMPD, COMP, COMP., COMPD, et cetera (etc.))2 or phrase. We only nest parentheses on The Road Rage to Lisp3 or when we are intoxicated beyond help.

We are very upsad that HyperText Markup Language (HTML) automatically collapses whitespace because we would prefer the opportunity to make a point of not joining sentences with multiple spaces.4

We always join the two numbers of a range with an en dash, and we only pad an en dash with spaces when we need to interrupt our ideas with inline asides because we have exhausted our endnote quota. We do only use em dashes to prefix a source under long quotations, without whitespace.

We consider it unbearably bad autistic style to start consecutive sentences or paragraphs with the same word or phrase, or to add clauses to sections where they clearly do not belong.

Notes & references

We use bidirectionally linked Arabic numerals incrementing from 1.

Endnotes

This is the way.

We do not use bibliographies, by the way.

We also consider it bad autistic style to end sentences or paragraphs with the same word or phrase, or to add clauses to sections where they clearly do not belong.

Citations

Academic citation styles are a giant hair ball of domain specific languages, invoking serious usability and accessibility problems. We have devised our own simplified format that hopefully is intuitive and compact enough to be both useful and readable.

Template placeholders on this page are marked with a bold sans-serif typeface. We never use bold type in body copy, with the exception of parody, satire and quotations.

Generic rules

  • Information is grouped with periods.
  • A group can be subdivided with a single comma.
  • With the exception of the cited source titles
    • We only use parentheses for the publication year.
      • A book’s edition is included before the year, separated with a comma.
    • We only use abbreviations for ordinal numbers.
    • We don’t use any quotation marks.
  • We use full public names for persons, initials if preferred or better known:
    • John F. Kennedy
    • Edward G. Robinson
    • H.G. Wells
  • A placeholder for persons can represent multiple full names joined by interpunction:
    • Joni Mitchell
    • Paul Simon & Art Garfunkel
    • David Crosby, Stephen Stills & Graham Nash
  • Since the author can contain a comma, we always use a period between author and title if the title is not linked to avoid ambiguity. Otherwise it depends on the contextual information.
  • We never use more than one link within a single citation. An endnote might contain additional text containing any number of links after the citation, though.

The following templates are complemented with examples that are generated with our Eleventy shortcode.5 If an example does not match its template, we probably introduced a regression.

Examples are grouped by source category for readability, their formats are not mutually exclusive.

Books

  • Author, Linked title (year).
    • Jane Doe, The Autistic Woman (2023).
  • Author, Linked title (ordinal edition, year).
    • Jane Doe, The Autistic Woman (12th edition, 2023).
  • Author, Linked title (year). Translated by translator.
    • Jane Doe, The Autistic Woman (2023). Translated by Hans Dampf.
  • Author. Linked title (year), page number.
    • Jane Doe. The Autistic Woman (2023), page 3.
  • Author. Linked title (year), pages number–number.
    • Jane Doe. The Autistic Woman (2023), pages 3–7.

The order of preference for linking a book depending on availability is

  1. the full source of the book (e.g. Gutenberg, archive.org).
  2. the author’s webpage about the book.
  3. the publisher’s webpage about the book.
  4. a Wikipedia article or article section about the book.

We never link to e-commerce platforms.

Anthologies

  • Author. Linked title, Anthology (year).
    • Jane Doe. The Autistic Woman, Women and Autism (2023).
  • Author, Linked title. Anthology (year), page number.
    • Jane Doe, The Autistic Woman. Women and Autism (2023), page 99.
  • Author. Title, Linked anthology (year).
    • Jane Doe. The Autistic Woman, Women and Autism (2023).
  • Author. Title. Linked anthology (year), page number.
    • Jane Doe. The Autistic Woman. Women and Autism (2023), page 99.

Note the difference between the examples with a page reference. There is always a period between author and title if the title is not linked.

Periodical articles

For linking articles, use the digital object identifier (DOI), if available.

  • Author, Linked title. Periodical, volume number (year).
    • Jane Doe, The Autistic Woman. Autism Today, volume 7 (2023).
  • Author, Linked title. Periodical, volume number (year). Translated by translator.
    • Jane Doe, The Autistic Woman. Autism Today, volume 7 (2023). Translated by Hans Dampf.
  • Author, Linked title. Periodical, volume number (month year).
    • Jane Doe, The Autistic Woman. Autism Today, volume 7 (March 2023).
  • Author, Linked title. Periodical, volume number (season year).
    • Jane Doe, The Autistic Woman. Autism Today, volume 7 (Spring 2023).
  • Author, Linked title. Periodical, volume number / issue number (month year).
    • Jane Doe, The Autistic Woman. Autism Today, volume 7 / issue 2 (Winter 2023).

We only include page numbers if no authoritative article source link is available.

  • Author. Title, Periodical. Volume number (year), page number.
    • Jane Doe. The Autistic Woman, Autism Today. Volume 7 (2023), page 42.
  • Author. Title, Periodical. Volume number / issue issue (year), page number.
    • Jane Doe. The Autistic Woman, Autism Today. Volume 7 / issue 2 (2023), page 42.

In rare cases, only the complete volume of the periodical is available online as a single resource. We include page numbers and link the periodical name, volume and year. Not supported yet as a shortcode.

  • Author. Title. Periodical, volume number (year). Page number.
  • Author. Title. Periodical, volume number / issue issue (month year) . Page number.

Webpages

Note: year must be the date of page content creation or the last significant update, if available. Do not use a generic page footer copyright date.

  • Author. Linked title, Website (year).
    • Jane Doe. The Autistic Woman, Autistic Bloggers (2023).
  • Author. Linked title, Website.
    • Jane Doe. The Autistic Woman, Autistic Bloggers.
  • Author, Linked title (year).
    • Jane Doe, The Autistic Woman (2023).
  • Author, Linked title.
    • Jane Doe, The Autistic Woman.
  • Linked title, Website (year).
    • The Autistic Woman, Autistic Bloggers (2023).
  • Linked title, Website.
    • The Autistic Woman, Autistic Bloggers.

TV shows

We only identifiy the writers.

  • Writer. Linked episode, Show. Season number, episode number (year).
    • Jane Doe. Pilot, BDSM-5. Series 1, episode 1 (2023).
  • Writer. Episode, Linked show. Season number, episode number (year).
    • Jane Doe. Pilot, BDSM-5. Series 1, episode 1 (2023).

We only identify the episode of a show with a single series.

  • Writer. Episode, Linked show. Episode number (year).
    • Jane Doe. Pilot, BDSM-5. Episode 1 (2023).

We refer to the entire show by providing its broadcasting year or year range.

  • Writer, Linked show (year).
    • Jane Doe, BDSM-5 (2001).
  • Writer, Linked show (year–year).
    • Jane Doe, BDSM-5 (2001,2003).
